1. Explain the difference between Analog and Digital Audio. expand_more

Analog Audio is a continuous signal that represents physical sound waves (e.g., sound recorded on a vinyl record or cassette tape). It captures the infinite variations of the sound wave.

Digital Audio is a discrete representation of sound waves. The continuous analog wave is sampled at intervals and converted into binary data (0s and 1s) for computer processing and storage.

2. What is Sampling Rate? expand_more

Sampling Rate refers to the number of samples of audio carried per second, measured in Hz or kHz. For example, CD quality audio has a sampling rate of 44.1 kHz, meaning the audio is sampled 44,100 times per second. Higher sampling rates generally result in better quality audio reproduction.
