Chapter 3: Audio and Video in Multimedia

This chapter explains how audio and video are important components of multimedia systems. It covers how they work, how they are stored, processed, and delivered to users.

1️⃣ Audio in Multimedia

Audio refers to sound, music, speech, and any audible information.

  • Types of Audio:
  • Analog audio — natural sound (e.g., human voice, instruments)
  • Digital audio — sound converted into binary form for storage and processing
  • Key Terms:
  • Sampling: Taking small pieces of the sound wave at regular intervals
  • Bit Depth: Determines sound quality (8-bit, 16-bit, 24-bit)
  • Sample Rate: Number of samples per second (e.g., 44.1 kHz)
  • Audio Formats:
  • MP3, AAC, WAV, OGG
  • Uses:
  • Background music, Narration, Sound effects, Voice commands

2️⃣ Video in Multimedia

Video is a sequence of images (frames) shown rapidly to create motion.

  • Key Terms:
  • Frame Rate (fps): Frames per second (e.g., 24fps, 30fps, 60fps)
  • Resolution: Clarity of the video (e.g., 1080p, 4K)
  • Compression: Reducing file size using codecs
  • Video Formats:
  • MP4, AVI, MOV, MKV
  • Video Codecs:
  • H.264, H.265, VP9
  • Uses:
  • Tutorials, Advertisements, Entertainment, Online courses

3️⃣ Importance of Audio & Video

  • Makes learning more interactive
  • Makes presentations more engaging
  • Delivers information faster and more clearly
  • Enhances user experience

4️⃣ Challenges

  • Large file sizes
  • Requires compression
  • Needs high processing power
  • Requires fast internet for streaming
